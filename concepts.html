Core Web3 Concepts

Comparing the building blocks of the decentralized web to traditional systems.

Web2

Centralized platforms owned by tech giants. Users trade their data for free services.

VS

Web3

Decentralized networks where users own their data and digital assets via cryptography.

Bitcoin

A decentralized digital currency (digital gold) primarily used as a store of value.

VS

Ethereum

A global supercomputer that runs smart contracts and powers decentralized applications (dApps).

Public Key

Like a bank account number. You share this to receive funds (your wallet address).

VS

Private Key

Like your PIN or password. Whoever holds this controls the assets. Never share it.

Traditional DB

Controlled by a single admin who can alter, delete, or censor data at will. (Client-Server)

VS

Blockchain

Distributed ledger validated by many nodes. Data is immutable, transparent, and append-only.
